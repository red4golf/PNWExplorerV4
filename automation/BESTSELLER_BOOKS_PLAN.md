# Popular Bestseller Books for Traffic Generation

## Strategy
Add one high-traffic bestseller to each location alongside the existing specialized books. This will capture searches for popular titles while maintaining educational authenticity.

## Top Bestsellers by Category

### Pacific Northwest General
- **"The Boys in the Boat" by Daniel James Brown** - #1 bestseller about 1936 Olympics from University of Washington
- **"The Good Rain" by Timothy Egan** - Classic Pacific Northwest travelogue, still highly popular
- **"Snow Falling on Cedars" by David Guterson** - Pulitzer Prize winner about Japanese American internment

### Lewis & Clark / Exploration
- **"Undaunted Courage" by Stephen E. Ambrose** - #1 bestseller with 7,000+ Amazon reviews
- **"Astoria" by Peter Stark** - Popular history of early Pacific Northwest exploration

### World War II / Japanese American
- **"Hotel on the Corner of Bitter and Sweet" by Jamie Ford** - 2.5 years on NYT bestseller list
- **"Farewell to Manzanar" by Jeanne Wakatsuki Houston** - Classic memoir, #1 in Teen History

### Manhattan Project / Atomic History
- **"American Prometheus" by Kai Bird & Martin J. Sherwin** - Pulitzer Prize winner, inspired Oppenheimer movie
- **"The Making of the Atomic Bomb" by Richard Rhodes** - Pulitzer Prize winner, definitive history

### National Parks / Hiking
- **"Hiking Mount Rainier National Park" by Mary Skjelset** - #1 bestseller in Mount Rainier books
- **"Best Easy Day Hikes" series** - Consistently popular hiking guides

### Military History
- **"The Guns of August" by Barbara Tuchman** - Classic military history bestseller
- **"Band of Brothers" by Stephen E. Ambrose** - Extremely popular WWII history

## Implementation Plan

### Phase 1: High-Impact Locations (Add These First)
1. **Japanese American Exclusion Memorial** → "Hotel on the Corner of Bitter and Sweet"
2. **Fort Clatsop** → "Undaunted Courage" 
3. **Hanford Site** → "American Prometheus"
4. **Mount Rainier** → "Hiking Mount Rainier National Park"
5. **Fort Casey/Worden/Flagler** → "The Boys in the Boat"

### Phase 2: Regional Bestsellers
6. **Pike Place Market** → "The Good Rain"
7. **Mount St. Helens** → "The Good Rain"
8. **San Juan Islands** → "Snow Falling on Cedars"
9. **Crater Lake** → "The Good Rain"
10. **Columbia River Gorge** → "Astoria"

### Phase 3: Category-Specific Bestsellers
- **All fort locations** → "The Boys in the Boat" (regional pride)
- **All natural sites** → "The Good Rain" (Pacific Northwest identity)
- **All WWII sites** → "Hotel on the Corner of Bitter and Sweet"
- **All exploration sites** → "Undaunted Courage"

## Benefits of This Approach

### Search Engine Traffic
- People searching for "Hotel on the Corner of Bitter and Sweet" will find your Japanese American sites
- "Undaunted Courage" searches will discover your Lewis & Clark locations
- "American Prometheus" searches will find your Manhattan Project content

### Cross-Pollination
- Fiction readers discover historical sites from their favorite books
- History buffs find related locations through popular titles
- Book club members discover field trip destinations

### Affiliate Revenue
- These bestsellers have much higher conversion rates
- Popular books have higher search volumes
- Established titles have proven purchasing patterns
